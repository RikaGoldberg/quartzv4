### Token Distribution:

About 94% goes into liquidity (so people can trade it), while you get 0.5%-1% as the creator (depending on whether you enable airdrop). If you enable airdrops, 0.5% is reserved for your community. If you don't enable airdrops, you get 1% as the creator (0.5% base + 0.5% from unused airdrop allocation). You can also pre-buy tokens at launch to boost the initial price. Lastly, 5% is allocated Over The Rainbow.

When you launch your token, 5% of the total supply goes "Over the Rainbow" which is our way of saying it's held on chain (for now) and will one day be used for Future Rainbow Community Distributions. This gets your token into more hands and gives the Rainbow community a stake in projects launched on the platform while keeping most of your token's supply (94%) in the liquidity pool for trading. As the creator, you still receive your share (0.5-1%) of the total supply, with an optional portion for airdrops to your community.

##### Liquidity Pool:

When people trade your token, a 1% fee is charged on each transaction. This fee is split between you (the creator) and the Rainbow Token Launcher protocol. You get 50% of these trading fees and the protocol gets 50%. These fees come from the liquidity pool that's created when you launch your token. The more trading volume your token generates, the more fees you earn! You can collect these fees anytime through the Rainbow app, with no minimum threshold required. It's a great way to earn passive income from your creation, even if you're not actively trading it yourself.

### Trading & Prices:

Token prices are initially determined by supply and demand through Uniswap liquidity pools. This is in place of a bonding curve. If more people are buying than selling, the price goes up. If more are selling than buying, it goes down. Think of it like a digital trading card - the value depends on how much people want it!

**Y**ou can trade any token that exists on supported networks through Rainbow's swap feature. Rainbow Token Launcher tokens can also be traded on other platforms that support ERC20 tokens.

### Common Questions:

1. _**What's "gas" and why do I need it?**_  
    Gas is like a transaction fee you pay to the network. You need some ETH (or whatever the network's native token is) to pay for gas when you launch a token or make trades.  
      
    [Learn more about gas here.](https://learn.rainbow.me/gas-fees-explained)  
      
    
2. _**Help! My transaction is stuck!**  
    _Don't worry! Transactions can sometimes take a moment depending on network conditions. Check your transaction status in Rainbow, and if you need help, our support team is just a message away.  
      
    [Learn more about fixing stuck transactions here.](https://rainbow.me/support/app/speed-up-a-transaction)  
      
    
3. _**What's the difference between swapping and bridging?**_
    
    - Swapping is trading one token for a different token on the same network
        
    - Bridging is moving your tokens from one network to another (like from Base to Arbitrum)
        
    
      
    [Learn more about swapping and bridging here.](https://rainbow.me/support/app/bridge-and-swap-tokens)
